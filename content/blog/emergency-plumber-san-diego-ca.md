## Frequently Asked Questions: San Diego Emergency Plumbing

**### Q: What qualifies as a true plumbing emergency?**
**A:** Any situation that poses an immediate risk of property damage, health hazard, or renders your home uninhabitable is an emergency. This includes: major leaks or flooding, burst pipes, no water at all, sewer backups, gas line leaks, and broken water heaters in winter. For minor drips or slow drains, you can often schedule a next-day appointment.

**### Q: I have a leak under my concrete slab foundation. How do you find it without tearing up my whole floor?**
**A:** We use non-invasive electronic leak detection technology. Our specialists use advanced listening devices, thermal imaging cameras, and pipe locators to pinpoint the exact source of the leak with remarkable accuracy. This allows us to make a single, targeted repair excavation, saving your floors and your budget.

**### Q: How does San Diego’s hard water affect my plumbing?**
**A:** Our water contains high levels of calcium and magnesium. Over time, these minerals form limescale buildup inside pipes, water heaters, and fixtures. This restricts water flow, increases pressure, reduces appliance efficiency, and accelerates corrosion, making pipes more prone to sudden failure. We can install whole-house water softeners to combat this pervasive issue.

**### Q: What should I do while waiting for your arrival during a major leak?**
**A:** First, locate your home’s main water shut-off valve (often near the street or in your garage) and turn it off. This stops the water flow. If safe, move valuables away from the water and place buckets under drips. For electrical safety, avoid standing water near outlets. If it’s a sewer backup, avoid using any sinks, toilets, or showers.

**### Q: Are your emergency rates higher than standard service?**
**A:** Emergency service requires immediate mobilization, specialized after-hours staffing, and priority parts sourcing, which does incur a premium. However, we believe in transparent, value-based pricing. We will always provide a clear, upfront estimate before any work begins, so you can make an informed decision. Preventing extensive water damage often saves you money in the long run.
