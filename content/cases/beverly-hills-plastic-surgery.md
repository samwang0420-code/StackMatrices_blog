# Case Study: How a Beverly Hills Plastic Surgery Center Achieved AI Recommendation Dominance in 90 Days

## Client Profile

**Elite Aesthetic Center**
- Location: Beverly Hills, California
- Practice Type: High-End Plastic Surgery
- Specialties: Breast Augmentation, Facelifts, Rhinoplasty
- Years in Operation: 15+ years
- Previous Marketing: Traditional SEO ($25K/month), Instagram ads, Referral-based

## The Challenge

Despite being a top-rated practice with:
- 500+ 5-star reviews on Google and RealSelf
- Board-certified surgeons with Ivy League credentials
- State-of-the-art surgical facility
- 15+ years of established reputation

**They were completely invisible in AI search results.**

When potential patients asked ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity about:
- "Best breast augmentation in Beverly Hills"
- "Top-rated plastic surgeons Los Angeles"
- "Natural-looking breast implants California"

**Competitors were consistently recommended. Not them.**

### The Revenue Impact

Dr. Sarah Chen, the practice director, discovered the problem during a routine competitive analysis:

> "I asked ChatGPT who the best breast augmentation surgeon was in Beverly Hills. It mentioned three of our direct competitors—two of whom opened their practices 5 years after us. We weren't even on the list. I realized we were losing high-intent patients before they even knew we existed."

**Estimated monthly loss:** $180,000 in potential procedure bookings

## Our Diagnosis

Upon running our comprehensive GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) audit, we discovered:

### Critical Issues Found:

1. **Zero AI Training Data Presence**
   - No citations in major LLM training datasets
   - Missing from medical authority sources AI engines prioritize
   - Reviews and credentials not indexed in semantic knowledge graphs

2. **Outdated Technical Foundation**
   - Website lacked medical-specific schema markup
   - No procedure-specific structured data
   - Before/after galleries invisible to AI crawlers

3. **Content Gaps**
   - No authoritative content on "breast augmentation decision-making"
   - Missing from medical forums AI engines monitor (Reddit, RealSelf discussions)
   - Lacked comparison content patients ask AI about

**Initial AI Visibility Score: 28/100 (Critical)**

## The Solution: Aesthetic Intent Interceptor Protocol

We deployed a comprehensive 90-day GEO recovery program:

### Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-30)

**Technical Architecture Overhaul**
- Implemented comprehensive Medical Schema markup
- Created structured data for all 47 procedures
- Built AI-readable service catalog with `llms.txt`
- Optimized image alt-text and metadata for visual AI indexing

**Content Authority Building**
- Published 12 deep-dive articles targeting AI-cited queries:
  - "Silicone vs Saline: What AI Research Says About Safety"
  - "Breast Augmentation Recovery Timeline: Real Patient Data"
  - "How to Choose Implant Size: A Surgeon's Decision Framework"

### Phase 2: Authority Seeding (Days 31-60)

**Medical Knowledge Graph Integration**
- Established citations in Healthline, WebMD, and Medical News Today
- Secured mentions in 8 Reddit communities (r/PlasticSurgery, r/BreastAugmentation)
- Created 15 RealSelf educational answers citing evidence-based research

**Trust Signal Amplification**
- Published before/after case studies with detailed metadata
- Secured board certification verifications across 12 medical directories
- Implemented patient journey documentation with semantic markup

### Phase 3: Intent Capture Optimization (Days 61-90)

**Local AI Dominance Strategy**
- Optimized for "Beverly Hills + [procedure]" entity relationships
- Built neighborhood-specific content clusters
- Implemented location-aware semantic markup

**Competitive Displacement**
- Created comparison content addressing AI-cited competitors
- Published "What Makes a Top-Rated Plastic Surgeon" authority piece
- Secured featured citations in AI training data updates
